# ALMA Recovery market wedge

Use when Mario explores receivables recovery, cobrança, FIDC competition, success-fee recovery, or adjacent "found money" opportunities.

## Core distinction

Do not collapse the idea into generic cobrança. The promising wedge is pre-FIDC, pre-jurídico, B2B, friendly receivables recovery:

- ALMA does not buy receivables.
- ALMA does not advance cash.
- ALMA does not become a FIDC/factoring vehicle.
- ALMA operates recovery and charges success fee on cash recovered.

The clean positioning:

> Before you sell the portfolio at a heavy discount or send it legal, let us recover it amicably. If money does not come back, you do not pay.

## Competitive map

### FIDC / securitization / factoring

Indirect competitors. They serve companies that want cash now or portfolio sale/advance. They prefer clean, large, standardized, documentable receivables with enough scale and predictable risk. Public market signals indicate thousands of FIDCs in Brazil, so the credit-rights market is large and validated.

ALMA wedge against FIDC:

- R$200K-R$3M overdue, too small/messy/relational for structured credit.
- Client wants to avoid selling at a steep discount.
- Invoices are recoverable with context and follow-up.
- B2B relationship preservation matters.

### Large collection/BPO players

Examples to consider in research: Paschoalotto, Bellinati Perez, Grupo Recovery, CRC, Liderança, Localcred, Atento/AeC/Konecta style operations.

They win on consumer volume, bank/retail/telco portfolios, standardized workflows, call-center scale, and low marginal contact cost.

ALMA wedge against them:

- Small/mid-size B2B portfolios.
- Higher-ticket invoices with context.
- Custom negotiation and tone preservation.
- Recovering without making the client's customers feel like mass delinquency records.

### Renegotiation marketplaces

Examples: Serasa Limpa Nome, Acordo Certo, QuiteJá, Meu Acerto, Blu365, QueroQuitar.

They are strong in consumer/CPF renegotiation, portal-based inbound, standardized discounts, and creditors with large portfolios.

ALMA wedge against them:

- Active outbound operation, not passive portal.
- B2B invoices and commercial context.
- Partial disputes, promises to pay, project history, relationship-sensitive recovery.

### Software / ERP / billing tools

Examples: ERP financial modules, Asaas, Omie, Conta Azul, Iugu, Vindi, Efi/Gerencianet, billing gateways, CRM cadences.

They send reminders and payment links, but the client still has to clean the data, prioritize, negotiate, call, follow promises, decide discounts, and close payment.

ALMA wedge against software:

> Your tool reminds. We operate.

### Legal collection

Law firms and protest/notification/judicial workflows are stronger once the debt is hard, old, disputed, or high-value enough to justify legal action.

ALMA wedge against legal:

> We enter before it becomes a dispute.

## ICP filters for pilot

Good pilot:

- B2B only.
- R$200K-R$3M overdue total.
- 20-300 debtors/accounts.
- Average ticket above R$2K.
- 15-180 days overdue.
- Clear proof of debt.
- Client wants relationship-preserving collection.
- Written discount/parceling authority.
- Success fee around 5-10%, often 7.5% as starting hypothesis.

Avoid:

- CPF consumer mass debt.
- Bank/fintech/card/telco/retail portfolios.
- Education mass collections.
- Very old debt.
- Judicialized debt.
- Purchased debt portfolios.
- Clients asking for aggressive or humiliating collection.

## Answering pattern

When Mario asks "what is the competition?", answer by layer:

1. The broad category is crowded.
2. FIDC is indirect competition, not same model.
3. Large BPO/collection players own consumer scale.
4. Marketplaces own consumer renegotiation.
5. Software owns reminders, not operation.
6. The attractive wedge is B2B, pre-FIDC, pre-legal, relationship-preserving success-fee recovery.

Keep the output decision-oriented: where to avoid, where to enter, and what contrast line to use against each competitor.
