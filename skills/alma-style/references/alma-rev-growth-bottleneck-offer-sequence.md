# ALMA Rev growth-bottleneck offer sequence

Use when drafting outbound for ALMA Rev around a diagnostic-style offer.

## Canonical pattern
- Offer line first, short and concrete
- Lead magnet in 1 page format, easy to skim
- Three email openers that each use a different angle, benchmark, teardown, risk
- CTA stays single and low friction, usually "Want me to send it?" / "Worth sending?"

## Useful framing
- Positive, opportunity-led language
- Prefer growth bottleneck, revenue engine, highest-leverage next move
- Avoid heavy consulting language and vague "assessment" framing when the goal is curiosity and reply

## Sequence
1. Offer line, 1 sentence
2. Lead magnet outline, 1 page
3. Email 1, benchmark angle
4. Email 2, teardown angle
5. Email 3, risk angle
6. If the user gives a short approval like "blz" or "segue", continue directly to the next artifact instead of re-explaining the plan

## Pitfalls
- Do not turn the first reply into a long strategy memo when the user only wants the next deliverable
- Do not add multiple CTAs
- Keep the body in English for ALMA Rev outbound assets
