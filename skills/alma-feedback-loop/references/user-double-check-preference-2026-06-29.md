# User preference, double-check and devil’s advocate

Session date: 2026-06-29

## What the user said
- Review the information he passes in.
- Research before accepting it.
- Check whether it makes sense.
- Correct him when the numbers or thesis do not hold.
- Act as the devil’s advocate for the company.

## Durable rule extracted
- Treat user claims, projections, and market numbers as hypotheses until grounded by research or operational evidence.
- If evidence does not support the claim, say so directly and explain why.
- Prefer the response shape: `Concordo / Não concordo / Por quê / Próximo passo`.
- In operational topics, end with a concrete next step and the metric that closes it.
- When the user asks to review a conversation for updates, extract stable preferences and workflow corrections into memory and the governing skill, not just into a transient answer.
